HI Comp 2024

The 1st What-is-HI Competition

The Pearls and Perils of Hybrid (Human-AI) Intelligence System Design


To be held in Malmö, Sweden, as a part of the HHAI 2024 competition.

Overview


Hybrid Intelligence (HI) is an emerging system design paradigm in which artificial intelligence (AI) augments, as opposed to replacing, human intelligence. Although there is an increasing emphasis on the idea of HI in the AI literature, there is a lack of systematic methods and metrics for developing HI systems.
The What-is-HI Competition (HI Comp) aims to support the development of high-quality HI (Human-AI) teams, by exploring the possible benefits, risks, and consequences of collaboration between humans and AI systems.

HI Comp aims at pushing the state-of-the-art in Hybrid Intelligence design, and at generating a first repository of scenarios for researchers and practitioners to guide the development and evaluation of HI teams.

Call for participation


HI Comp delves into an interdisciplinary subject and requires minimal prerequisite technical expertise for participation.
We invite participation from all segments of the HHAI community.
All participants will be able to participate to the competition directly during the HHAI 2024 conference in Malmö, Sweden, in June 2024, without prior registration.

Challenge: Fundamental qualities of HI teams


The theme of this first iteration of the HI Comp is: fundamental qualities of HI teams.

The main task for the participants of the competition is to formulate HI scenarios that yield best and worst quality HI teams. The participants are presented with a set of use-cases involving HI teams. Analyzing these use cases, the participants are asked to develop best and worst case usage scenarios, based on the quality attributes from a recent Quality model for HI (Human-AI) Teams (see image below). The scenarios, reported in a specific template, are then evaluated by a jury of HI experts. The participants are ranked based on the best and worst case scenarios they formulate.

Rules

  • Participants form teams of two–three.
  • Participants are provided with a description of one or more HI use cases and a number of HI quality attributes (such as the HI team capability to communicate, coordinate, and identify other members, or the HI team awareness of team norms).
  • Participants must submit detailed usage scenarios highlighting the benefits and risks stemming from the lack of (some of the) characteristics.
  • Usage scenarios should be described in a given format (template provided to the participants). The participants may optionally include visual aids or multimedia elements.
  • A jury composed by the competition organizers will evaluate the scenarios based on the evaluation criteria (see below)

Evaluation criteria

The scenarios will be evaluated based on:
  • Creativity
  • Complexity
  • Realism
  • Potential impact
  • Clarity of presentation

Prizes

Certificates and monetary prizes will be presented to the top three teams.
  • 1st place: € 500
  • 2nd place: € 400
  • 3rd place: € 300
